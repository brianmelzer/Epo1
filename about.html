Our Story · Since 1926

A house forged in 1926

The story of FERVEUR begins in 1926, in a narrow atelier along a side street in Le Marais, where a young craftsman named Henri Laurent hammered his first carbon steel pan by hand. Paris was hungry, its brasseries were legendary, and Henri believed the tools behind every great kitchen deserved the same artistry as the dishes they produced.

For decades, the Laurent atelier supplied the kitchens of Paris quietly — pan by pan. Each piece was forged from a single sheet of steel, finished by hand, and stamped with the family crest. Cooks did not replace a Laurent pan. They inherited it.

As the world turned to coatings and convenience, the forge fell quiet, and the name nearly disappeared. FERVEUR is its revival — the same atelier, the same obsession, the same belief that the everyday pan should be the finest object in the kitchen. A forgotten French house, rediscovered.

The Atelier

Drawn in Paris. Forged in Sweden.

In our atelier, every curve, angle and proportion is refined until it feels inevitable.

The Ritual

Caring for carbon steel

Three habits, and your Ferveur will outlive every other pan in the kitchen.

01

Cook With It

Use it often, and use fat. Every sear builds the seasoning — the dark, naturally non-stick patina that defines great carbon steel.

02

Clean It Warm

Rinse warm, wipe dry, skip the soap when you can. A quick scrub of coarse salt lifts anything stubborn without stripping the surface.

03

Oil & Rest

A thin film of oil over gentle heat before it cools, and your pan is sealed and ready for the next flame.
